# Disentangled Degradation Representation via Triple-Latent Variational Attention for Underwater Image Enhancement

Underwater images are essential for marine exploration and autonomous systems, yet they are frequently compromised by complex degradation, including colour distortion, blurred details, and uneven illumination caused by light absorption and scattering. Addressing these challenges requires enhancement methods that can adapt to the one-to-many nature of degradation, where a single input can correspond to multiple plausible high-quality outputs. Existing deep learning approaches often rely on deterministic mappings, limiting their adaptability in dynamic underwater environments. Here we propose TLVNet, a novel framework that introduces a Triple-Latent Encoder (TLE) to explicitly model and decouple three independent degradation factors—colour shift, texture detail loss, and illumination imbalance—as distinct Gaussian latent spaces via variational inference. This representation enables targeted, controllable enhancement for each degradation type. To further improve feature representation and reconstruction quality, we incorporate a Hierarchical Attention Module (HAM) that progressively refines multi-scale features, and a Multi-Gate Refinement (MGR) module that employs a gating mechanism for effective deblurring and noise suppression. Extensive experiments on four benchmark underwater subsets (UIEB-T140, EUVP-S235, EUVP-T515, and UFO-T120) demonstrate that our method achieves superior visual quality and competitive quantitative performance. For instance, on the challenging EUVP-T515 dataset, TLVNet attains a PSNR of 29.681 dB and an SSIM of 0.904. By providing a more interpretable and flexible enhancement pathway, this disentangled representation framework offers a significant step toward more robust and intelligent underwater vision systems.
